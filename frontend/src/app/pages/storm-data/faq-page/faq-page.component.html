Frequently Asked Questions

The default map view shows your property’s surge risk. If you have chosen another layer and want to return, use the radio buttons at the bottom left corner of the map to select “Storm Surge” and you will see your surge risk information appear on the upper left side of the map.

When the “Storm Surge” map view is active, the upper left side of the screen will display a house icon color coded accorded to your property’s surge risk, the forecasted peak surge height, the forecasted peak surge time, and the current distance between the storm center and your property. Below the property specific information, current storm advisory information from the National Hurricane Center (NHC) is displayed. The following is visible on the map: color-coded house icon at the location of your property, a contoured layer showing the forecasted storm surge, the NHC track and cone (if selected), and a line labeled with the current distance from the center of the storm to your property.

Yes.

The color of the house icon corresponds to your property’s surge risk according to the “Property Surge Risk” Legend. If your property is not expected to experience surge and no surge is forecasted nearby, the house icon will appear white. If surge is forecasted in your area, but your property sits at an elevation above the forecasted surge height or is protected by levees, the house icon will appear yellow. If surge is predicted at your property, but the peak surge is forecast to be below 3 feet, the house icon will appear orange. If the surge is forecast to rise above 3 feet at your property, the house icon will appear red.

The storm surge layer shown on the map is the expected water level above mean sea level, while your property surge risk is specific to your individual property. If your address is above mean sea level (most are), the surge experienced at the property will be lower than the surge above mean sea level. For example, if the surge above mean sea level is predicted to be 10 ft, but the ground elevation of the property is 6 ft, the peak surge predicted at your property will be 4 ft.

Yes.

The color of the house icon corresponds to your property’s wind risk according to the “Property Wind Risk” Legend. For wind, there are 7 different levels of risk. If maximum sustained winds are forecast to stay below tropical storm force at your property (<39 MPH), the house icon will appear white. If maximum sustained winds are forecast to reach tropical storm force or above, the house will be colored according to the NHC tropical cyclone category of the forecasted peak wind value (Tropical Storm to Category 5 Hurricane). For more information about the types of damage caused by each category of wind, please visit the NHC’s Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale page at https://www.nhc.noaa.gov/aboutsshws.php.

Q-risq Analytics begins forecasting operations for an active storm approximately 5 days before a predicted landfall on the U.S. Gulf of Mexico or Atlantic coastlines. For information about a storm greater than 5 days out, or forecast to make landfall outside of our service area, please visit the National Hurricane Center.

The wind and surge risk is updated every 6 hours based on the forecasted storm track and intensity from the National Hurricane Center and Q-risq’s storm surge and wind model results. If the risk to your property has changed, there has likely been a change in the forecast. The farther from landfall, the more common it is for the forecasted path and intensity to change. Please check back frequently to get the most up-to-date information.
